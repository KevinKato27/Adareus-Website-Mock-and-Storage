Meet the Co-Founders

Keith Kato
Co-Founder
Life Sciences GMP Ops IMS Supply Chain OpEx

Keith brings more than 20 years of expertise in operations management across life sciences, specialty retail, and aerospace. Most recently, he served as Vice President of Operations at FUJIFILM Diosynth Biotechnologies, where he led site operations for a leading CDMO.

Prior to FDB, Keith led operations at Atara Biotherapeutics to improve GMP compliance and ensure clinical supply. As VP of Technical Operations, he spearheaded the Atara COVID-19 program, built a 33,000 sq ft research facility, and delivered a successful EU pre-approval inspection.

Keith has held key leadership positions at Alexion and Amgen, and earlier in his career led production at Blue Nile and served as Team Lead at Honeywell. BA from UC Berkeley, MBA from USC.

Daniel Lee
Co-Founder
Biotech QA / QC OpEx Analytics Cell Therapy

Daniel brings more than 17 years of multi-gavel experience in life sciences, spanning internal and external manufacturing, CDMO operations, quality assurance, quality control, supply chain, and operational excellence. Most recently Head of Operational Excellence at Atara Biotherapeutics.

Prior to Atara, Daniel held roles of increasing responsibility at Alexion, Amgen, and Allergan — responsible for mAb manufacturing, HPLC testing of BOTOX®, and JAPAC launch lead for multiple Amgen products including BLINCYTO® and Prolia®.

Daniel's expertise centers on applied analytics, Management Systems for Operations, and QMS design across multiple biotech sites. BS in Biochemistry from UC San Diego, MBS from Keck Graduate Institute.

Robert Uyekawa
Co-Founder
TPS / Lean Toyota Kaizen Multi-building

Robert brings more than 30 years of expertise across life sciences, automotive, aerospace, construction, and food. A widely recognized Toyota Production System and Lean expert, he began his career at Toyota Motor Sales, USA — receiving specialized training in Japan in project management, tooling, and production operations.

At Toyota, Robert held key roles across manufacturing, quality, supply chain, and supplier development. Over the past 15 years, he has partnered with leading organizations in biotech, medical devices, aerospace, and automotive to implement TPS philosophy and continuous improvement practices.

His work builds the internal discipline required to sustain operational improvement over the long term — not just achieve it once. BS from Cal Poly Pomona.
